Valorant Agent Tier List — March 2026 (Patch 10.04)

March 13, 2026 · Updated same day as patch · Season 2026 Act 1

TL;DR — March 2026 meta in three bullets

  1. Clove is the best agent in the game — not just the best controller. Her self-revive ult and decaying smokes make her oppressive at every rank. If she's not banned, pick her.
  2. Phoenix is back — 50.60% win rate after buffs. Least expected comeback in the meta. He's a legitimate entry fragger now, not just a grief pick.
  3. Vyse is the sentinel meta — Highest pick rate at VCT Masters Bangkok. If you're playing sentinel, this is your agent.

Meta Overview — March 2026

The current Valorant meta rewards agents with high utility density and self-sufficiency. Smokes that do more than smoke (Clove, Omen), initiators who gather information and create picks (Fade, Sova), and sentinels with map-wide presence (Vyse, Killjoy) dominate ranked play. Pure fraggers without utility get punished unless you're at the absolute peak of their mechanical ceiling.

The two biggest surprises entering this patch: Phoenix is legitimately viable after two rounds of buffs, and Sage — previously viewed as a liability above Gold — is now an A-tier pick after her Slow Orb was buffed to affect dash abilities.

Duelist Tier List

Duelists need to open sites and win duels. In the current meta, the best duelists are those who can both entry and provide value when behind.

TierAgentsNotes
SJett, Phoenix, ReynaJett's Tailwind remains one of the best mobility abilities in the game — unmatched for Operator plays and aggressive repositioning. Phoenix at 50.60% WR after buffs: Flash is faster, Hot Hands heals more aggressively. Reyna excels when you can win gunfights, with instant LOS denial and healing after every kill.
ANeon, Iso, RazeNeon's speed creates angles opponents can't prefire. Iso's one-on-one dueling is almost unfair at higher ranks — Shield and undercut make him a menace. Raze has the highest damage ceiling of any duelist; paint shells punish grouped-up defenses.
BYoru, WaylayYoru requires extreme game sense and communication to maximize — genuinely A-tier in coordinated teams. Waylay is a newer agent still being figured out by the playerbase; her movement-based kit is high-skill-floor but rewarding.
CDeadlock (Duelist)Better categorized as a sentinel in practice. Her kit doesn't enable the aggressive entry play duelists need to succeed.

Phoenix comeback story: He went from "never pick Phoenix" to legitimately viable in two patches. His Blaze wall now provides real cover when rotating, Hot Hands heal is more aggressive on activation, and Run It Back's ult cost dropped. At 50.60% win rate he's not just viable — he's recommended.

Controller Tier List

Controllers win rounds by denying information and forcing enemies into unfavourable positions. Clove has completely reshuffled this category.

TierAgentsNotes
SClove, OmenClove is the best agent in Valorant right now — not just the best controller. Her smokes decay after placement (you must stay alive to maintain them), but Meddle's decaying fragment buys you time even when you die, and her passive grants temporary HP from nearby deaths. The ult lets her revive if she gets kills when it's active. Omen's shadow teleport and paranoia remain the most map-wide controller kit in the game.
AViper, Astra, HarborViper dominates specific maps (Icebox, Breeze, Haven) with unmatched one-way smoke setups. Astra's global presence makes her invaluable in coordinated play — harder to use in ranked, but ceiling is high. Harbor excels at A-site takes with a wall that physically blocks movement.
BBrimstoneEasiest controller to learn — three independent smokes, a molly, and a stim beacon. His ult has a long windup but is excellent for closing rounds. Great starting point for learning the controller role, but gets outscaled by Clove and Omen above Plat.

Initiator Tier List

Initiators gather information, flash for entry fraggers, and create space. The best initiators are those who can operate independently rather than relying on team follow-through.

TierAgentsNotes
SFade, Sova, BreachFade's Prowlers track targets through smokes — information gathering that adapts to enemy positioning rather than requiring pre-knowledge. Sova's Recon Bolt + Drone cover more map per ability than any other initiator. Breach's stun and flash through walls make him devastating when paired with fast-executing duelists.
AKAY/O, Gekko, SkyeKAY/O's suppression is the anti-agent kit — shutting down abilities is uniquely powerful against Clove-heavy comps. Gekko's Wingman can plant or defuse, adding a unique dimension to default rounds. Skye's flash has the best turning arc of any flash in the game; her heal is bonus.
BTejoA newer initiator still being figured out. His Guided Salvo ability is powerful but requires coordination to maximize. High ceiling in organized play, harder to use effectively solo.

Sentinel Tier List

Sentinels anchor sites and gather passive information. The best sentinels create value even when you're not watching them.

TierAgentsNotes
SVyse, KilljoyVyse had the highest sentinel pick rate at VCT Masters Bangkok. Her Arc Rose can lock an enemy in place and disable their weapon, and her Shear wall provides one-way angles on nearly every map. Killjoy's low learning curve belies her power: Nanoswarm, Turret, and Lockdown create a real "no entry" zone that hard-counters fast site hits.
ASage, CypherSage's Slow Orb now affects dash abilities (Phoenix Tailwind, Jett Tailwind) after a Patch 9.10 buff — a major upgrade against the current duelist meta. Self Heal up to 50. Cypher's Trip Wires and Spycam give the best passive map coverage of any sentinel, especially on Split and Ascent.
BDeadlock, ChamberDeadlock's kit is punishing but requires opponents to overextend into it. Chamber had his global travel removed and lost his identity — playable, but a shadow of what he was.

MapMust-PickWhy

Comp Templates That Win Right Now

  • High-info default: Jett / Fade / Sova / Clove / Killjoy — aggressive info + two smokes + anchor sentinel. Works on every map.
  • Fast execute: Neon / Breach / KAY/O / Omen / Vyse — suppression into stun into Neon speed, Omen smokes, Vyse locks post-plant angles. Requires team coordination but suffocates the defense.
  • Control play: Phoenix / Skye / Fade / Viper / Cypher — two-layer information with Phoenix popping flashes from cover. Map-dependent but dominant on Bind and Icebox.
