What is actually live today

The official April 10 update is the 1.2x Candy Eggs Weekend. Sugarfest gameplay stays live through Monday, April 13, and the Sugarfest Wagon starts on April 13 and stays until April 17. Uplift also confirmed that Gumball Caterpillars are the only pets not available in the wagon.

What changed in today's update

Today is a clean final-weekend update, not a brand-new pet wave. The important part is the timing: players get 20% extra Candy Eggs on the last Sugarfest weekend, then the event shifts into a wagon phase on April 13. That gives you one final grind window before the event becomes a pure spend-and-cleanup phase.

The official notes also settled the biggest question about what disappears: Gumball Caterpillars will not be in the wagon. If you are already close to five Gumballs, this weekend is the moment to finish that grind. For almost everything else, the final spend window stretches to April 17.

One very important exception

Gumball Caterpillar is not a Candy Egg purchase, but it is still the single most urgent Sugarfest target this weekend because Uplift explicitly said it will not be in the wagon. If you are at three or four Gumballs already, finishing those laps is more important than buying another cosmetic.

How to spend your Candy Eggs this weekend

1. Pet-first players should buy missing pets and pet-progress items before cosmetics

If you are missing any of the Sugarfest pets that can be bought or built with Candy Eggs, those should come first. That means Choccybunny Box, Cocoadile, Jiggly Jerboa, Candy Chisels for Gummy Guana, and Mochi Mallets for Mochi Meow. The reasoning is simple: pets are the part of Sugarfest most likely to matter later in trading and collection goals, while most lower-rarity cosmetics are easier to skip without regret.

2. Choccybunny Box is the cleanest pure Candy Egg gamble

If you want the best pet-focused Candy Egg sink, the Choccybunny Box is the cleanest answer. One purchase gets you a guaranteed Sugarfest pet, and the upside path is the Dark Choccybunny. It is the most straightforward way to turn a 15,000-CE chunk into a pet outcome rather than a cosmetic one.

3. Use the wagon for cleanup, not indecision

Once the wagon opens on April 13, the job is to clean up your leftover balance, not to keep hesitating. If you already have the pets you want, that is the right time to pivot into items like Inspector Sherbet Bus, Sour Glider, Gummy Biplane, or the best Ultra-Rare wear pieces. The wagon exists so you do not lose leftover Candy Eggs; treat it like a final spend window, not a reason to postpone every decision until Monday.

4. Good budget tiers if you are not sure what to do

1
Under 5,000 CE

Grab cheap collection pieces like Twisty Marshmallow, Gumball Paws, Sticker Pack, Waffle Coat, or Pink Sprinkle Goggles. At this balance, completion and style are the sensible goals.

2
5,000 to 15,000 CE

Target one meaningful mid-ticket item such as Candihorn Instrument, Cocoadile Hat, Gummy Aviator Scarf, Whipped Cream Hat, or save to 15,000 for a Choccybunny Box.

3
15,000 to 60,000 CE

This is the strongest pet-and-collector range. Boxes, Cocoadile, Jiggly Jerboa, and progress items should be decided first. Only move into higher-end cosmetics after the pet goals are settled.

4
60,000+ CE

You can start thinking about one big flex piece like Sour Glider or Inspector Sherbet Bus, but it still makes sense to finish any pet goals first unless you are purely collecting event cosmetics.

Best spend order if you want the short version

  1. Finish Gumball Caterpillar progress this weekend if you are close.
  2. Spend Candy Eggs on missing pets or pet-progress items before cosmetics.
  3. Use Choccybunny Box as the main 15,000-CE pet gamble.
  4. Save the wagon phase for leftover balance cleanup and high-cost flex items.
  5. Only buy low-rarity accessories early if you genuinely want them for collection, not because you think they are the best trade-value use of Candy Eggs.
