Marvel Rivals Hero Tier List — Season 1.5 (March 2026)

March 13, 2026 · Season 1.5 mid-season patch · Season 2 launches April 11

TL;DR — Season 1.5 meta in three bullets

  1. Magneto is the best tank in the game — his Metal Bulwark shield can absorb 800+ damage per cast, and his ult locks an entire team in place for easy follow-up. Pick Magneto into any composition.
  2. Elsa Bloodstone is the most underrated Duelist — 51.8% win rate at Diamond+. Her Nym Rounds debuff amplifies all damage the target takes, making her a team-damage multiplier rather than just a solo fragger.
  3. Luna Snow is still broken — her Fate of Both Worlds ult grants team-wide invincibility for 3 seconds AND damages enemies simultaneously. Nothing else in the Strategist role comes close to her team-fight presence.

Meta Overview — Season 1.5

The Season 1.5 mid-season patch buffed Groot, nerfed Hawkeye's headshot multiplier, and introduced Gambit as a new Duelist. The dominant team composition remains a double-Strategist setup (Luna Snow + Invisible Woman is the current best duo), with Magneto + Groot as the premiere anchor pair for Vanguards. Season 2 launches April 11 with a major meta reset, so this tier list is specifically for climbing in the remaining weeks of Season 1.5.

One structural note: Marvel Rivals' Team-Up abilities can shift hero value significantly depending on team composition. Where a Team-Up creates a significant power spike, it's called out specifically in the tier notes below.

Vanguard Tier List (Tanks)

Vanguards create space, absorb damage, and enable dives. The best Vanguards right now are those who can do this independently rather than requiring perfect coordination from their team.

TierHeroesNotes
SMagneto, GrootMagneto's Metal Bulwark is the best ability in the tank role — absorbs projectile damage, protects the entire team, and generates passive Ultimate charge. His ult (Meteor M) pulls all nearby enemies into a central point, enabling instant team wipe potential. Groot received a significant mid-season buff: his Thornlash Wall now regenerates 15% of its HP per second rather than requiring kills. He's now a genuine anchor tank who can hold a choke point indefinitely.
AThor, Captain America, HulkThor's Storm Surge gives the team insane vertical mobility — elevator to high ground instantly. His Mjolnir throw deals 120 damage on return-path (often ignored by opponents). Captain America's Shield Toss into Charging Star is still the best engage pattern in the game for forcing fights on the tank's terms. Hulk is in A tier after his transformation changes — Worldbreaker Hulk mode now lasts 12 seconds instead of 8, making his ult fight-decisive rather than just disruptive.
BVenom, Doctor Strange, Peni ParkerVenom has a high skill ceiling but punishing floor — his symbiote mobility is best in class, but a bad engage often means a dead Venom with your team in 4v6. Strange's Sling Ring portal remains uniquely powerful in organized play; harder to maximize in ranked without communication. Peni Parker's web nest setup is legitimately oppressive on defense-heavy maps like Yggdrasil but falls apart on open map layouts.
CBlack Panther (Vanguard build)Black Panther works better as a Duelist. The Vanguard build sacrifices his kit's core strength (burst mobility and assassin pressure) for a tankiness that other characters do more naturally. Only worth considering in very specific dive-heavy counter compositions.

Best Vanguard Team-Up: Magneto + Storm (Duelist) — their "Magnetic Conductor" Team-Up lets Magneto's Metal Bulwark redirect 20% of absorbed damage back as a chain lightning pulse through Storm's presence. In practice this means your shield deals passive AoE damage while protecting your team. Extremely strong on grouped enemy comps.

Duelist Tier List (DPS)

Duelists win team fights by dealing concentrated damage. In Season 1.5, the best Duelists are those who can burst targets and then survive to reset — pure glass cannons without escape options have fallen off.

TierHeroesNotes
SElsa Bloodstone, Black Widow, GambitElsa Bloodstone: Nym Rounds debuff is the single best damage amplifier in the game — 20% increased damage taken by the target for 4 seconds on every Nym hit. She enables double-Duelist compositions where both DPS hit the same target simultaneously for massive burst windows. Black Widow: headshot-focused kit with best mobility in the Duelist role — grapple resets on kill, meaning a team fight can become a full squad wipe solo. Gambit: NEW this season. His kinetic card charges deal 165 damage each at max stacks and his ult (Royal Flush) scatters charged cards in a fan pattern — instant fight-ender in choke points.
AIron Man, Winter Soldier, StormIron Man's Repulsor Blast + Unibeam combo is still one of the highest damage outputs in the game — his weakness is lack of a dash or excape tool, so positioning is everything. Winter Soldier's bionic arm throw pierces multiple targets and applies a slow — invaluable for locking targets for your tank to follow up. Storm's aerial presence is unmatched; her Omega Hurricane ult repositions her anywhere on the map, enabling plays that no other Duelist can make.
BSpider-Man, Magik, Scarlet WitchSpider-Man has the highest skill ceiling in the entire game — literally unplayable in ranked without 20+ hours of practice, but top 500 Spider-Man players are genuinely untouchable. Magik's teleport-heavy kit is A-tier in organized play; B-tier solo queue. Scarlet Witch received a nerf to her Reality Erasure ult radius (reduced by 15%) but is still viable as a zoning Duelist.
CHawkeye, Moon KnightHawkeye's headshot multiplier nerf (from 2.5x to 2.0x) directly gutted his one-shot potential. He's playable but no longer the dominant sniper threat that defined early Season 1 competitive. Moon Knight's Ankh system is hard to maximize in the chaos of ranked play — enemy teams rarely stand still in his orb placement zones.

Strategist Tier List (Support)

Strategists win games by keeping their team alive and amplifying their team's damage. The double-Strategist meta means your two support picks carry enormous weight — choosing wrong is a one-way ticket to a lost team fight.

TierHeroesNotes
SLuna Snow, Invisible WomanLuna Snow: Fate of Both Worlds (ult) is the single most impactful ability in the game. 3 seconds of team-wide invincibility + AoE damage means you can absorb an enemy ult, ult back, and win a fight you had no right to win. Her passive healing aura at 40+ HP/sec is also the best sustained healing in the Strategist role. Invisible Woman: her Force Physics push-pull is the best peel ability in the game — she can eject diving Duelists off your backline with one ability. Her Invisibility Field on teammates enables plays that shouldn't be possible (fully invisible tank engages, invisible Duelist flanks).
AMantis, Loki, Adam WarlockMantis: Sleep Spores remains the best crowd-control ability available to the Strategist role. A well-placed sleep in a team fight resets the entire engagement. Her healing output is lower than Luna's but her CC contribution makes up for it in coordinated play. Loki: his clone system creates the most effective fake-out in ranked — enemies burn mlts on Loki clones constantly. His healing is passive-area-based and underestimated by opponents. Adam Warlock: his Soul Bond passive resurrects an ally on kill — the only permanent resurrection mechanic in the game. Exceptionally powerful in close-fought matches where a single pick decides the round.
BRocket Raccoon, Cloak & DaggerRocket's B.R.B. ult is theoretically the second-best ult in the game (mass resurrection) but requires 6 seconds of vulnerable stationary positioning to activate — gets interrupted almost every time in ranked without peel. Strong in highly coordinated play. Cloak & Dagger's dual-mode healing and damage is unique but the mode-switching creates cognitive load that makes her harder to master than her stats suggest.
CJeff the Land SharkJeff's Joyful Splash healing is reliable but his kit lacks any hard CC or amplification. He's a pure healer in a meta that rewards Strategists who can do more than heal. His ult (It's Jeff!) is fun but requires enemies to cluster near water, which rarely happens at higher ranks.
