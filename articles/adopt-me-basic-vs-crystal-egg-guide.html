Basic Egg vs Crystal Egg: where should you spend your tickets?

These are the two main ticket eggs players compare constantly. The Basic Egg is cheap and broad, while the Crystal Egg costs ten times more but massively improves the odds of pulling something premium. The right choice depends on whether you care about volume or top-end efficiency.

Reviewed by The Patch Staff using the current Patch egg directory and public methodology pages.
Basic Egg: 400 Tickets Crystal Egg: 4,000 Tickets Both live in rotation

Quick verdict

If you want the best chance at premium pulls, Crystal Egg is the clear winner. If you want more total hatches, broader collection coverage, or you are on a much smaller ticket budget, the Basic Egg still makes sense. This is really a premium-efficiency question, not a simple which-egg-exists question.

Best use of this page

Use this guide when you are deciding whether to keep rolling cheap eggs or save up for a concentrated premium run. It is one of the most practical egg decisions on the site.

Why Basic Egg still matters

  • Low entry cost: You can hatch much more often, which is useful if you are filling out your collection or just want more rolls.
  • Broader pool: The Basic Egg covers commons through legendaries, so it feels more like a general collection egg.
  • Lower pressure: Bad runs feel much less painful because each opening is so much cheaper.

Why Crystal Egg is the premium play

  • Legendary odds jump: Crystal Egg takes legendary odds from 0.26% up to 6%, which is an enormous difference.
  • No commons or uncommons: Your ticket spend is concentrated into rare-and-up hatches, which is much better for premium chasing.
  • Cleaner premium hunt: If you are saving for high-end pulls, this is the more efficient destination.

Who should buy Basic Egg?

Players who want more total hatches, budget-friendly opening sessions, and broader collection coverage. It is the better egg when you care about participation volume more than concentrated premium odds.

Who should buy Crystal Egg?
