How to read the list

Raw value is only part of a real trade. The best traders also pay attention to demand strength, liquidity, and collector premiums.

Demand matters

Owl, Cow, and Hedgehog routinely trade above what casual players expect because demand stays stronger than pure rarity would suggest.

No-potion can be a premium

On high tiers, no-potion is not a downgrade. Collector demand can push it above the default trade version, so the site now treats it as its own lane.

Confidence is honest

Stable old pets usually get higher confidence. Faster-moving recent pets get a little more caution, even when their raw values look strong.

Methodology

The goal is to be clear about where the numbers come from and what they are good for.

Why not use a simple Neon equals 4x rule?

Because the market does not behave that way. Some neons trade below a perfect 4x multiple, some above it, and no-potion collector variants can break those rules entirely.

Are these values exact?

No. They are benchmarks reviewed against trusted community references. Real servers still shift based on demand, urgency, and how easy a pet is to move.

Why start with benchmark pets first?

Because benchmark pets anchor the trades that most players care about. Once those are consistent, the rest of the catalog can be added without breaking trust.
