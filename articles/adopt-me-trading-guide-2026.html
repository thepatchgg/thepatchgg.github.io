How to Trade in Adopt Me! 2026 — Complete Beginner's Guide

FR multipliers, fair trade principles, scam prevention, and a step-by-step trading ladder from common pets to legendaries.

Updated March 21, 2026·18 min read·The Patch Staff

The Basics: How Trading Works

To trade in Adopt Me!, walk up to any other player, tap the Trade icon above their head (or use the menu), and both players select up to 4 pets to offer. Both players must tap Accept for the trade to complete. You can also add in-game Bucks to sweeten an offer.

No real money changes hands in a trade — only in-game pets and Bucks. Anyone offering Robux "outside the game" in exchange for your pets is running a scam.

Most important rule: Never trade anything you can't afford to lose. Always verify the exact items on both sides of the trade window before you tap Accept.

Understanding Pet Value: The 5 Layers

1

Rarity Tier

The base value layer. Legendary > Ultra Rare > Rare > Uncommon > Common. A Legendary will almost always be worth more than an Ultra Rare, but within the same rarity, other factors dominate.

2

Potion Status (Normal vs. FR)

This is the biggest value multiplier most new traders miss. A Fly Potion lets a pet fly. A Ride Potion lets you ride the pet. Both potions are consumable — once used, they're gone. An FR (Fly + Ride) pet is permanently enhanced and is worth significantly more. See the FR multiplier table in our Value List.

3

Age (Full-Grown matters)

A pet needs to be Full-Grown before it can become a Neon. Most traders prefer Full-Grown pets over Newborn/Junior/Pre-Teen/Teen/Post-Teen because they're ready to Neon immediately. Always check the age when a trade involves "value pets."

4

Neon and Mega Neon

A Neon requires 4 Full-Grown versions of the same pet combined. It glows with colored light. A Mega Neon requires 4 Full-Grown Neons (= 16 regular pets total). Neons are worth roughly 4–6Ã an FR version; Mega Neons are 4–5Ã a Neon. The rarer the base pet, the bigger the Neon premium.

5

Demand & Market Timing

Two pets with identical base value can trade very differently based on current demand. An event pet during its release week is easy to trade. The same pet six months later may require more negotiation. Check our value list's demand arrows to know whether to buy, hold, or sell.

The Trading Ladder — From Nothing to Legendary

This is how experienced traders climb from basic pets to high-value legendaries over time. Patience and applying FR potions at the right stage are the keys.

Stage 1
Collect FR Commons & Uncommons Open free eggs and apply Ride + Fly potions to your best Common and Uncommon pets. FR Uncommons are worth 6–12Ã Normal Uncommons — this is your first real value boost.
Stage 2
Trade up to FR Rares Trade 3–4 FR Uncommons for 1 FR Rare. Repeat until you have 2–3 FR Rares, then combine into an FR Rare Neon (requires 4 FR Rares of the same species).
Stage 3
Cross into Ultra Rares A Neon FR Rare or 3–4 FR Rares can get you an FR Ultra Rare. FR Sharks, FR Crabs, and similar Ocean/Farm egg Ultra Rares are good targets here.
Stage 4
Enter B-Tier Legendaries From here, trade toward B-tier legendaries: FR Axolotl, FR T-Rex, FR Kitsune. These are liquid and let you step into the mid-legendaries economy.
Stage 5
Climb to A-Tier and Beyond With 2–3 B-tier FR legendaries, you can trade toward A-tier: Cerberus, Queen Bee, Kangaroo, Crow. From there, the path to Giraffe, Owl, and eventually Shadow Dragon is clear — just slower.

Buy when demand is low, sell when it peaks

Event pets are easiest to acquire during Week 1 of an event (prices haven't peaked yet). The same pet is harder to trade for by Week 3 when everyone wants it. Sell/trade away event pets in the last few days of an event, when buyers are desperate and will overpay.

Post-event appreciation window

Limited pets almost always dip slightly in the first 2 weeks after an event ends (people are relieved they got what they wanted and trade them away). Then they start rising as supply locks in permanently. The best long-term buys are in this 2-week post-event window.

Scam Prevention — Red Flags to Know

    Trust Trading
    Someone asks you to trade your pet to them first, promising to trade back + something extra after. This is always a scam. The official trade window exists exactly so you don't need to trust anyone. Never trade outside of it.
  • ð
    Pet Quick-Swap
    In fast trades, a scammer shows you a high-value pet, then swaps it for a lower-value one right before you accept. Always read the full trade window carefully before tapping Accept — don't rush.
  • ð
    Fake Value Claims
    "This pet is worth way more than yours, you're getting a great deal." Always check an independent value list (like this one) rather than trusting the other trader's claims about their own pet's value.
  • ð°
    Robux for Pets
    Robux cannot be transferred through trades. Anyone offering Robux in exchange for your pets — via Discord, YouTube comments, or in-game chat — is lying. Report and block immediately.
  • ð®
    Duplication Glitch Claims
    "Send me your pet and I'll duplicate it and return two." There are no legitimate pet duplication glitches. This is always a scam. Anyone claiming to have one is stealing your pet.

    Phishing Links
    Never click links sent by other players claiming to show your "trade value" or "account status." These are phishing sites designed to steal your Roblox login. Only use the official Roblox website.

Advanced Tips

ð¡

Apply potions before trading, not after

FR potions are worth more than the pets they go on at lower rarities. Apply a Ride Potion and Fly Potion to a Rare or Ultra Rare pet before trading it — you'll often get 3–5Ã more value than if you traded the Normal pet and the potions separately.

ð¡

Trade in bulk during low-traffic hours

Server activity is highest after school hours (3–8pm local time). More traders = more competition for good deals. Try trading during off-peak hours when there's less competition and more motivated sellers.

ð¡

Specialize — pick one or two pet lines to master

Top traders don't try to know every pet. They deeply understand a narrow range (e.g., Ocean Egg pets, or Halloween event pets) and exploit their knowledge advantage. If you know the Turtle is slightly undervalued right now and most casual traders don't, you have a real edge.

ð¡

Watch what streamers and influencers are trading

Demand in Adopt Me is heavily influenced by YouTube and TikTok. When a popular creator showcases a specific pet, demand spikes within 24–72 hours. If you can get ahead of these trends — by holding a diverse portfolio of limited pets — you can benefit from sudden demand surges.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you trade in Adopt Me?
Walk up to another player and tap the trade icon, or use the in-game Trade tab. Both players select up to 4 pets to offer. You can also add in-game Bucks. Both must accept before the trade completes. No real money is involved — only in-game items.
How do you know if a trade is fair?
Compare both sides using a value list. Check rarity, potion status (FR vs Normal), and demand trend. A fair trade has values within ~15% of each other. Use our March 2026 Value List as your reference.
What is a good starter trade for beginners?
Collect free eggs, hatch Common and Uncommon pets, apply FR potions to the best ones, then trade up. Your first real target should be an FR Rare — something like an FR Bunny or FR Cow. From there, work toward FR Ultra Rares and eventually mid-tier legendaries.
Can you get scammed in Adopt Me?
Yes, but scams are avoidable. Never trust-trade (give your pet expecting something back outside the trade window). Always read the full trade offer before accepting. Never click links from other players. Robux cannot be transferred through trades — anyone offering Robux for pets is lying.
Is there a best time to trade in Adopt Me?
Off-peak hours (early morning or late night in your region) often have more motivated sellers and less competition. During events, the first and last days of an event are the most liquid. Event pets are easiest to offload when an event is still live — harder to sell after it ends.
