LoL Tier List Patch 26.5: Best Champions by Role

March 13, 2026 · Updated same day as patch · Next patch: 26.6 — March 26

TL;DR — Patch 26.5 in three bullets

  1. Junglers get buffed across the board — Lee Sin, Lillia, Nocturne, and Volibear are all stronger. If you play jungle, this is your patch.
  2. Mid lane gets hit hard — Orianna, Azir, Taliyah, and Neeko were dominating pro play. All nerfed. Ahri and Mel become better alternatives.
  3. Kha'Zix punished in solo queue — Base movement speed down 5, Q AD ratio trimmed. He's still playable but no longer feels oppressive.

Patch 26.5 Meta Overview

This is the First Stand patch — the first major international tournament of 2026. Riot usually keeps pro-facing changes measured for tournament patches, but the mid lane got hit hard regardless. Four of five nerfs target mid laners who've been dominating pro drafts for weeks. Meanwhile, four of six buffs target junglers, making this the strongest jungle meta in recent memory.

The result: aggressive, early-skirmishing junglers dominate, with supports and top laners picking up slack in a mid lane meta that's in flux.

Jungle Tier List

SLee Sin, Nocturne, Rek'Sai, LilliaAll buffed or newly viable. Lee Sin Q damage up at every rank — his early invades are more punishing than ever. Nocturne's global pressure is exceptional in a skirmishing meta.

Jungle MVP — Lee Sin: Q base damage goes from 60–180 to 65–185 across both hits, meaning a full Q combo lands +10 damage at every rank. In a game decided by early fights and river control, that number matters. Play Lee Sin if you can execute the mechanics. Play Nocturne if you can't.

Mid Lane Tier List

SAhri, Mel, Veigar, BrandAhri is the meta winner. Versatile, safe, scales well — and her recent buffs make her the single most consistent mid pick. Mel gets targeted buffs toward mid after pro-scene frustration dialed back. Veigar and Brand profit from the assassin nerfs.
ASyndra, Lux, Viktor, Cassiopeia, ZedAll remain strong with no changes. Zed benefits from Azir/Orianna nerfs — fewer waveclear walls in the meta. Viktor reliable into most matchups.
BOrianna, Azir, Taliyah, Neeko, Twisted FateOrianna's Q cooldown up 1s early — real laning phase nerf. Azir loses 187 HP by level 18, giving assassins actual windows to punish. Neeko's utility still real but numbers trimmed.
CGalio, Yone (mid), LeblancGalio struggles in the current carry meta. Yone better in top lane. Leblanc punished by the improved jungle aggression — less room to roam.

Patch 26.5 Winners & Losers

Winners ↑

  • Lee Sin: Q base damage up on both hits. The most impactful single-champion buff this patch. Play him in jungle.
  • Lillia: Riot reversed the 25.19 ult buff and 26.02 Q nerfs — net improvement. She's back to feeling fluid and her damage output returns to where it was before the tinkering.
  • Nocturne: Minor stat improvements pushed him from B to S-tier in the context of a skirmish-heavy meta where his global ult punishes overextended midlaners who just got nerfed.
  • Garen: Base stat improvements. Simple champion that now simply performs better. Ideal for players who want to mute chat and focus on CS and side lanes.
  • Mel: After recent gameplay rework reduced frustration, Riot brings her numbers back up. Now viable mid with a genuine identity.
  • Volibear: Consistent minor buff improves clear speed and dueling. Jungle Volibear is now legitimately threatening pre-6.

Losers ↓

  • Orianna: Q cooldown up 1 second at early ranks. This is purely a laning phase nerf — she can no longer bully opponents out before they scale. Still strong late, but easier to play against in the first 10 minutes.
  • Azir: Health growth drops from 119 to 108 per level. At level 18, that's 187 less HP — a real window for assassins and divers to punish him in mid-to-late game. He's still a pro-tier pick, but solo queue players should lean elsewhere.
  • Kha'Zix: Base movement speed down 5 (350→345), Q bonus AD ratio down 5% (110%→105%). Two nerfs targeting solo queue's most oppressive jungler. Still viable in skilled hands but no longer the obvious best choice.
  • Varus: Lethality build gets adjusted. He was the most pro-dominant ADC in the pre-patch meta. Drops from S-tier to B-tier in most role-by-role evaluations.
  • Taliyah & Neeko: Both nerfed for pro play dominance. Taliyah loses wave clear consistency. Neeko utility numbers trimmed. Both still viable but outclassed by cleaner alternatives.

One-Line Verdict by Role

  • Top: Play Ornn or Garen. Don't be a hero.
  • Jungle: Lee Sin if you can play him; Nocturne if you can't.
  • Mid: Ahri or Mel — both buffed, both consistent, neither banned every game yet.
  • ADC: Jinx. Point the gun and shoot.
  • Support: Taric for the WR ceiling, Leona for the pub-stomp.
