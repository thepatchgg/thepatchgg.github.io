🦊 Latte Kitsune — Legendary Pet

The Latte Kitsune is a limited Legendary pet available for 600 Robux (~$7.50 USD) in the Robux shop starting March 27. It's a coffee-themed fox with kitsune (Japanese fox spirit) styling — one of the more distinctive-looking Legendaries in recent Adopt Me history.

How to Get the Latte Kitsune

The Latte Kitsune is a direct Robux purchase — no Candy Egg grind required. Head to the event shop after the March 27 update and buy it for 600 Robux. It will be available until April 6, 2026, after which it becomes trade-only.

🔥 Should You Buy the Latte Kitsune?

At 600 Robux (~$7.50), this is the most affordable Robux Legendary in Sugarfest. Candicorn was 1,000 Robux. Lower price means higher accessibility and more supply, but also strong demand from casual players who love the fox design. Event Legendaries with Robux pricing tend to appreciate steadily post-event as new players seek them through trading. It's a reasonable hold if you like the aesthetic.

🐀 Jiggly Jerboa - Common Pet

The Jiggly Jerboa is a limited Common pet priced at 6,000 Candy Eggs. Don't overlook it because of its Common rarity - Commons make the most accessible Neons and Mega Neons, and a well-designed limited Common can hold surprising value in Neon form post-event.

How to Get the Jiggly Jerboa

Earn Candy Eggs through the Jerboa Jam minigame, Game of Sweets board, and other event activities, then spend 6,000 in the event shop. The Jerboa is earnable purely through gameplay - no Robux required.

💡 Get Multiple Jerboas for Neon

A Common Neon requires 4 pets, and a Mega Neon requires 16. At just 6,000 Candy Eggs each, you can collect all the Jerboas you need for a Neon within a day of solid Jerboa Jam grinding. The Neon version is where the real value builds.

🎮 Jerboa Jam Minigame - Full Guide

Jerboa Jam is a timed maze minigame that launched with Week 3. Players control a pet and race through a maze, collecting as many cheesecakes as possible before the timer runs out. The more cheesecakes you collect, the more Candy Eggs you earn - making it the primary new farming activity for the final week of Sugarfest.

How to Access Jerboa Jam

Head to the Candy Cliffs area on Adoption Island. The Jerboa Jam entry point is located near the base of the cliff ladder. Walk up to the entry portal and wait for the next round to begin. Matches run on a timed cycle - you'll be teleported in automatically when the timer hits zero.

How to Play

  1. Enter the Maze When teleported in, you control your pet character through a multi-path maze. A timer appears at the top of your screen showing how long you have to collect cheesecakes.
  2. Collect Cheesecakes Cheesecakes are scattered throughout the maze - run over them to collect. More cheesecakes = more Candy Eggs at the end of the round. Prioritize high-density areas and paths with clusters.
  3. Race Other Players Multiple players compete in the same maze simultaneously. Cheesecakes disappear once collected, so faster routes and knowing spawn locations give you a meaningful edge.
  4. Collect Your Earnings When the timer ends, your Candy Egg total is tallied based on cheesecakes collected. Eggs are added to your event pile on the riverbank.

Jerboa Jam Tips & Strategies

  • Learn the layout fast - The maze has fixed walls. After 2-3 runs you'll know the optimal cheesecake route. Muscle memory is the biggest edge.
  • Prioritize dense clusters - Some corridors have 4-5 cheesecakes in a row. Hit those first before other players clear them.
  • Stack runs - Jerboa Jam runs frequently. Back-to-back sessions during a dedicated grinding hour are your fastest route to 6,000 eggs for a Jiggly Jerboa.
  • Combine with Game of Sweets — Play the daily board game between Jerboa Jam cooldowns to maximize total Candy Egg income.
🏆 Best Candy Egg Farm Strategy

For fastest Candy Egg grinding: play Jerboa Jam every round + hit the Game of Sweets Jackpot space daily (500 eggs + 4 dice) + complete pet needs for the 200-egg bonus between sessions. Dedicated players running Jerboa Jam consistently can earn 3,000-6,000+ Candy Eggs per hour.

🧗 Candy Cliffs - New Area

Week 3 opened the Candy Cliffs - a vertical zone on Adoption Island accessed via a ladder leading up to a cave. The Cliffs host the Jerboa Jam entry point, the Gummy Guana unlock mechanism (7 x Candy Chisels), and additional event interactables.

📍 Finding the Candy Cliffs

From spawn, head toward the outer edges of Adoption Island. Look for the candy-striped rock formation with a ladder attached. Climb it to reach the Candy Cliffs cave area where the Jerboa Jam portal and Gummy Guana station are located.

💰 Candy Egg Priority - Week 3 Edition

With the event ending on April 6, here's how to prioritize your remaining Candy Eggs across all three weeks of content:

  1. Top Priority: Mochi Meow (if you haven't yet) - 125,000 Candy Eggs for the only effort-based Legendary of the event. If you're close but not there, Jerboa Jam gives you a new earning avenue. Highest long-term value of all event pets.
  2. Second Priority: Jiggly Jerboa (multiple for Neon) - At just 6,000 eggs each, this is the most egg-efficient pet in the entire event. Grab 4+ to make a Neon. Common Neons of limited event pets have consistently strong post-event value.
  3. Third Priority: Waffle Wyrm, Robux pets, cosmetics - Waffle Wyrm (80K) is still a solid Ultra-Rare. Candicorn and Latte Kitsune are Legendary if you're willing to spend Robux. Cosmetics (Sour Glider, Inspector Sherbet Bus) hold value as unique wearables but are lower priority than pets.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Is Latte Kitsune or Mochi Meow the better Legendary to get?

They serve different purposes. Mochi Meow requires Candy Egg grinding (125K eggs) but no Robux, and will likely have lower supply post-event due to the effort required. Latte Kitsune is a fast 600 Robux purchase. Both are Legendary and will be trade-only after April 6. If you have the Robux and like the Kitsune design, it's worth buying. If you're free-to-play, Mochi Meow through grinding is your Legendary path.

Should I still bother with Jiggly Jerboa if it's just a Common?

Yes - limited Common pets from events are among the most accessible sources of Neon material. At 6,000 eggs for a Neon-ready Common, you can realistically build a Neon Jiggly Jerboa (24,000 eggs total) in a few good sessions. Neon versions of well-designed Commons consistently hold value in the trading community.

How much does 600 Robux cost in real money?

600 Robux costs approximately $7.50 USD when bought at standard rates (400 Robux = $5). Roblox sometimes sells Robux bundles at a slight discount, so check if any bonus packs are active before purchasing.

What happens to my Candy Eggs after the event ends?

A vendor wagon stays open until April 10 with remaining purchasable event items (except the Candy Chisel). After April 10, Candy Eggs are no longer spendable. Make sure you've converted everything you want before that date.

Is this the last week of Sugarfest?

Week 3 (Jerboa Jam) was the final content update for Sugarfest 2026 — Week 4 dropped April 4 with Chocolate Bunny, Pupcake, and the Easter Bunny. The event fully ends April 10.
