Week 3 at a Glance

Two new pets land March 27 — Latte Kitsune (Legendary, 600 Robux) and Jiggly Jerboa (Common, 6,000 Candy Eggs). A brand-new minigame called Jerboa Jam launches alongside a new Candy Cliffs area. The event ends April 6, so this is the final week to earn Candy Eggs.

What's New in Week 3

Sugarfest Week 3 — officially titled Jerboa Jam — drops on March 27, 2026 at 11:15am ET and is the third and final content wave of the Sugar Festival event. Here's a breakdown of everything confirmed before launch:

  • 2 new limited pets: Latte Kitsune (Legendary) and Jiggly Jerboa (Common)

🦊 Latte Kitsune — Legendary Pet

The Latte Kitsune is a limited Legendary pet available for 600 Robux (~$7.50 USD) in the Robux shop starting March 27. It's a coffee-themed fox with kitsune (Japanese fox spirit) styling — one of the more distinctive-looking Legendaries in recent Adopt Me history.

How to Get the Latte Kitsune

The Latte Kitsune is a direct Robux purchase — no Candy Egg grind required. Once the update drops on March 27, head to the event shop and purchase it for 600 Robux. It will be available until April 6, 2026, after which it becomes trade-only.

🔥
Should You Buy the Latte Kitsune?

At 600 Robux (~$7.50), this is the most affordable Robux Legendary in Sugarfest. Candicorn was 1,000 Robux. Lower price = higher accessibility = more supply, but also more demand from casual players. Event Legendaries with Robux pricing tend to appreciate steadily post-event as new players want them through trading. It's a reasonable hold if you like the design.

🐭 Jiggly Jerboa — Common Pet

The Jiggly Jerboa is a limited Common pet priced at 6,000 Candy Eggs. Don't overlook it because of its Common rarity — Commons make the most accessible Neons and Mega Neons, and a well-designed Common can hold surprising value in Neon form post-event.

How to Get the Jiggly Jerboa

Earn Candy Eggs through the new Jerboa Jam minigame, Game of Sweets board, and other event activities, then spend 6,000 in the event shop. The Jerboa is earnable purely through gameplay — no Robux required.

💡
Get Multiple Jerboas for Neon

A Common Neon requires 4 pets, and a Mega Neon requires 16. At just 6,000 Candy Eggs each, you can collect all the Jerboas you need for a Neon within a day of solid Jerboa Jam grinding. The Neon version is where the real value builds.

🎮 Jerboa Jam Minigame — What We Know

The Jerboa Jam is a brand-new maze-based minigame launching with Week 3. Players control a pet and race through a maze, collecting as many cheesecakes as possible before time runs out. The more cheesecakes you collect, the more Candy Eggs you earn — making it the primary new farming activity for the final week of Sugarfest.

Confirmed Details

  • Format: Timed maze race — collect cheesecakes before the timer expires
  • Currency earned: Candy Eggs (rate TBD until launch)
  • Player control: You control a pet character through the maze
  • Competitive element: Multiple players race simultaneously — collect more than your opponents

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Is Latte Kitsune or Mochi Meow the better Legendary to get?
They serve different purposes. Mochi Meow requires Candy Egg grinding (125K eggs) but no Robux, and will likely have lower supply post-event due to effort required. Latte Kitsune is a fast 600 Robux purchase. Both are Legendary and will be trade-only after April 6. If you have the Robux and like the Kitsune design, it's worth buying. If you're a free-to-play player, Mochi Meow through grinding is your Legendary path.
Should I still bother with Jiggly Jerboa if it's just a Common?
Yes — limited Common pets from events are among the most accessible sources of Neon material. At 6,000 eggs for a Neon-ready Common, you can realistically build a Neon Jiggly Jerboa (24,000 eggs total) in a few good sessions. Neon versions of well-designed Commons consistently hold value in the trading community.
How much does 600 Robux cost in real money?
600 Robux costs approximately $7.50 USD when bought at standard rates (400 Robux = $5). Roblox sometimes sells Robux bundles at a slight discount, so check if any bonus packs are active before purchasing.
What happens to my Candy Eggs after the event ends?
A vendor wagon stays open until April 10 with remaining purchasable event items (except the Candy Chisel). After April 10, Candy Eggs are no longer spendable. Make sure you've converted everything you want before that date.
Is this the last week of Sugarfest?
Yes. Week 3 (Jerboa Jam) is the final content update for Sugarfest 2026. No new pets will be added after March 27 — this is your last chance to earn all event pets in-game before April 6.
